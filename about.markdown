I graduated summa cum laude from The Ohio State University in 2023 with my B.S. in Sociology and a minor in Computer and Information Science. I've worked on a wide variety of research projects, ranging from the study of racial wealth gaps to public opinion of fentanyl overdose victims. 

I'm currently a pre-doctoral fellow at the [Poverty and Inequality Research Lab][PIRL] at Johns Hopkins University, where I assist with quantitative and qualitative studies examining housing access, social mobility, education, and more. To learn more about PIRL's work, visit their [website][PIRL].

## Professional fun facts
- **I majored in computer science for the first two years of college,** then converted it to a minor once I discovered my love for sociology. Because of this, I'm particularly fond of quantitative research and interesting computational methods.
- **I've held a lot of interesting jobs and internships,** including stints as a digital content intern at a PBS/NPR station, the Director of Operations of a [harm reduction nonprofit](wwww.thesoarinitiative.org), a research assistant in an [epidemiology lab](wwww.ndews.org), and a software engineering intern at a trucking company.
- **I chose to do a pre-doctoral fellowship to hone in on my research interests.** At PIRL, I'm able to learn both qualitative and quantitative methods, and we study everything from housing to education. 

## Personal Fun Facts
- **I'm a passionate fan of public transit and an even more passionate hater of cars.** You'll often find me waiting at the bus stop for Baltimore's Charm City Circulator, our free (and chronically unpredictable) bus. 
- **I love to crochet, and I have an Etsy store.** I sell crochet patterns, which provides me with some extra income as I prepare to (hopefully) live on a PhD stipend for the next few years.
- **I was born and raised in Cincinnati, Ohio.** It's an incredibly underrated city in an incredibly underrated state. No matter where I live, I'll stand firmly by that position.
